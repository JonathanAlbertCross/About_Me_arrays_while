Top 10 favourite novels

  1. "The Stranger"
    (L'Étranger) by Albert Camus
    An existentialist novel that follows the life of Meursault, a detached and apathetic French Algerian who becomes embroiled in a murder case.

  2. "To the Lighthouse"
    by Virginia Woolf
    A modernist masterpiece that delves into the inner thoughts and experiences of the Ramsay family as they vacation on the Isle of Skye.

  3. "The Brothers Karamazov"
    by Fyodor Dostoevsky
    A philosophical and psychological novel that explores the moral dilemmas and relationships among the Karamazov brothers.

  4. "Ulysses"
    by James Joyce
    A complex and experimental novel that explores the life of its protagonist, Leopold Bloom, over the course of a single day in Dublin.

  5. "Faust"
    by Johann Wolfgang von Goethe
    This epic drama in two parts tells the story of Faust, a scholar who makes a pact with the devil, Mephistopheles, in exchange for knowledge and worldly pleasures.

  6. "Buddenbrooks"
    by Thomas Mann
    A family saga that spans several generations, this novel examines the decline of the Buddenbrook family in a changing society.

  7. "The Trial"
    (Der Prozess) by Franz Kafka
    A classic of existential literature, this novel explores themes of bureaucracy and alienation as Josef K. is arrested and subjected to an absurd and opaque legal process.

  8. "The Reader"
    (Der Vorleser) by Bernhard Schlink
    A novel that examines the relationship between a young man and an older woman with a dark secret, set against the backdrop of post-World War II Germany.

  9. "Siddhartha"
    by Hermann Hesse
    A spiritual novel that follows the journey of Siddhartha, a young man who seeks enlightenment and self-discovery in ancient India.

  10. "The Nun"
    (La Religieuse) by Denis Diderot
    This novel tells the story of Suzanne Simonin, a young woman forced into a convent against her will. It explores themes of religious oppression, abuse, and the struggle for autonomy.
